Process Design Tag versus Functional Design Tag

1 August 2011, revised 7 August 2011, 11 August 2011

Introduction

As discussed in the topic Functional and Physical Objects - the big picture we know Process Design and Plant Design. In Process Design we design the processes in terms of instances of ClassOfActivity (e.g. PUMPING, HEAT EXCHANGING, DISTILLING) and instances of ClassOfInanimatePhysicalObject of which instances of Stream are members.

This is done with process analysis software, and results in documents like a PFD (Process Flow Diagram), Heat and Material Balances, and Process Data Sheets for equipment. That equipment is, at this stage, still a functional abstraction, a ClassOfActivity. The next stage is to define suitable Technical Solutions, as discussed in forementioned topic. In the diagram below P-101 is the technical solution for process tag P24, as it is located at hydraulic network node J-1.

Attributed Information

In the diagam below a beginning has been made with explaining what information can be attibuted to the various objects (more to follow):

 

The stream STRM-593092 participates in the pumping activity ACT-345642 at node J-1. The set of stream data, of which we show only one here, is composed into the Heat & Material Balance. These stream data are stored at class level. The individual activity and stream, ACT-345642 and STRM-593092, represent information about a process simulation.

Based on this the Process Engineer creates his Process Data Sheet for (here) the function place P-101. This is not a plain copy of the stream data, but includes other requirements, such as the required overcapacity.

These "process conditions" (minimum, normal, maximum, etc) are entered in the Pump Specification for (here) P-101.
